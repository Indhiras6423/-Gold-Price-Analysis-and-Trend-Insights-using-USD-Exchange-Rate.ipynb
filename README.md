## 📋 Project Summary

This Power BI dashboard analyzes **Gold and USD price movements** from January 2020 to April 2026 using Yahoo Finance data. The project explores gold price trends, market phases, volatility patterns, and the inverse relationship between gold and the US Dollar Index (USD).

The dashboard covers **1,582 trading days** across **5 distinct market phases**: Pre-COVID, COVID, Inflation, Recovery, and Surge.

Gold delivered an exceptional return of **+218.6%** over the study period — rising from **$1,524.50 (Jan 2020)** to **$4,857.60 (Apr 2026)**. The Surge phase was the dominant market condition, while the COVID period recorded the all-time low of **$1,477.30 on March 18, 2020**.

## 💡 Key Insights

### 1. 🚀 Exceptional Gold Returns
- Gold surged **+218.6%** from $1,524 (Jan 2020) to $4,857 (Apr 2026) — nearly **tripling in 6 years**
- All-time high of **$5,318.40** reached on January 29, 2026 during the Surge phase
- Consistent year-on-year growth except during the COVID crash in March 2020

### 2. 🏆 Surge Phase Dominates the Market
- Surge phase had the highest trading days — **723 days** (full dataset)
- Within 2020–2025 filter, Surge recorded **650 days** — still the longest phase
- Driven by geopolitical uncertainty and inflation fears from mid-2023 through 2026

### 3. 🦠 COVID Crash — The Lowest Point
- Gold hit all-time low of **$1,477.30 on March 18, 2020** during COVID market panic
- Strongest single-day inverse: **March 13, 2020** — USD +0.72%, Gold -4.63% same day

### 4. ⚡ Volatility Patterns
- **High Volatility:** 540 days — concentrated in COVID (2020) and Surge (2024–2026)
- **Low Volatility:** 521 days — mostly during stable Inflation and Recovery phases
- High volatility periods coincide with COVID, Fed rate hikes, and geopolitical crises

### 5. 🔗 Gold–USD Relationship
- Overall positive correlation of **0.80** — Safe-Haven demand dominates
- **369 days (23.3%)** showed true inverse behavior — USD up, Gold down same day
- During geopolitical crises, both Gold and USD rise simultaneously — breaking the inverse rule

## 🌐 Why USD Affects Gold — 4 Core Forces

| # | Force | Explanation |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 01 | 🌍 **Global Pricing in USD** | Gold is priced globally in USD. When dollar strengthens, gold appears cheaper — dampening demand. When USD weakens, gold price rises globally. |
| 02 | 🛡️ **Inflation Hedge** | As USD loses purchasing power, investors turn to gold as a store of value. This relationship has held for centuries across currency crises. |
| 03 | 📊 **Interest Rates** | When Fed raises rates, bonds yield more — reducing appeal of non-yielding gold. Lower rates push investors toward gold. |
| 04 | 🏦 **Safe-Haven Demand** | During geopolitical crises, investors flee to BOTH gold and USD simultaneously — temporarily breaking the inverse correlation. |
